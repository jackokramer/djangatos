More about the Cats

Djan~Gatos noun Django Cats (Spanish Gatos), or jazz musicians (cats) partial to the French Gypsy Jazz stylings of Django Reinhardt.

Steeped in a love all for all things jazzy, Djangatos strives to take your grandpa's favorite genre into the 21st century by blending 100+ years of jazz into a unique mixture, presented through the lens of a Hot Club acoustic group. Merely a vehicle for the music of a much larger culture, Djangatos performances feature jazzy outfits, historical trivia, and lots of chances for the listener to learn and laugh. The group encourages attendees to come dressed in period garb, and audiences are always urged to interact and sing along, hopefully leaving the show as Djangatos disciples that will spread the good word of Django Reihardt and the deep gypsy jazz idiom.

How We Started

Vocalist and Lead Guitarist Robert Alan Witmeyer always dreamed of starting a gypsy jazz group, and was sitting on a bunch of material he'd written in the hot club style. While working at Keith Holland Guitars in Los Gatos CA, he saw a unique gypsy acoustic guitar in for repair. When the owner came in to retrieve his repair, Robert exclaimed, "That guitar looks like we should be friends," leading to a long-last friendship between Robert and guitar owner, Ron La France.

Already a local percussionist, Ron La France was interested in learning more about the Gypsy genre, and began taking lessons from Robert. Soon they co-founded the Los Gatos Gypsy Swing Club, and hosted monthly meetings in the Keith Holland Guitars Recital Room. The meetings featured some of the most notorious musicians of the genre, giving Witmeyer and La France ample opportunities to study and djam. The duo was fortunate to glean knowledge from Paul Mehling (the Godfather of American Gypsy Jazz), Samson Schmitt (son of Dorado, and one of the best in that idiom), Douglas Martin, Alex Hand, and Vic Wong. Out of these monthly Djams, Djangatos was born.

Searching for a name, Robert and Ron found many of the obvious choices were taken. In a moment of brilliance, La France suggested Djangatos, combining Django and Gatos. The name was a nod to Django and the town where they originally met, but also a reference to Robert's previous swing group, Martini Max and the Swing Kats (gatos).
